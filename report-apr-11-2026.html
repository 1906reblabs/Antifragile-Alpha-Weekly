The US announced 145% tariffs on Chinese goods on April 9 — the highest tariff rate since the Smoot-Hawley Act of 1930. China was explicitly excluded from the 90-day pause extended to other trading partners. This is the critical nuance: the trade war is concentrated on the US-China axis. South Africa exports only ~8% of goods to the US. Our primary export partner is China (~15%) and the EU (~25%).

Three things happened simultaneously: leveraged gold ETF holders faced margin calls and sold gold (driving USD gold from $4,700 to $3,100). The rand weakened 14% on oil-driven inflation fears and EM risk-off. Sasol's coal-to-liquids economics deteriorated as Brent hit $63 — our hard stop at R148 was triggered and executed.

The antifragile framework separates liquidity-driven selling (temporary, creates opportunity) from fundamental deterioration (persistent, requires exit). The gold drop is the former. The Sasol drop is the latter. Acting differently on each is what creates the edge.

"The antifragile investor does not panic at regime changes — they recalculate. Every forced selloff is the market gifting you the positions it cannot hold."

Key barbell actions this edition: Exit SOL (stop triggered, no re-entry this cycle). Raise gold miners to 20% (ZAR gold thesis). Add Capitec (CPI) as anti-crisis banking entry. Add Kumba Iron Ore (KIO) on China stimulus anticipation. Raise rand-hedge mandate to 45%. Reduce moonshot to 5% given elevated volatility.

Trade-War-Aware Screening Methodology ✓ Free Preview

Our core multi-factor model (Value/Quality/Momentum/Catalyst/Liquidity) is unchanged. Three new trade war overlays dominate this edition's reweighting:

  • ZAR gold conversion filter (mandatory from this cycle): Any SA gold miner must be evaluated on ZAR gold received, not USD gold. At R19.2/USD, every dollar of USD gold weakness is partially offset by rand depreciation. The ZAR gold price is what drives SA miner earnings — and that price (R59,500/oz) remains extraordinary vs AISC of R30,000–35,000/oz.
  • China domestic stimulus anticipation: US tariff escalation historically triggers Chinese domestic infrastructure stimulus within 60–120 days. We've seen this pattern in 2009, 2018, and 2019. Infrastructure = steel = iron ore. We position Kumba Iron Ore ahead of this announcement, not after it.
  • Rand-hedge mandate raised to 45%: At R19.2/USD, purely domestic SA equity faces structural headwinds. We require ≥45% of the portfolio to earn in USD-equivalent terms: gold miners (USD revenue, ZAR costs), offshore-proxies (NPN), and commodity exporters (KIO, AMS).
  • Liquidity-vs-fundamental distinction: Gold dropped $1,600 in one week due to margin-call liquidation, not because the world suddenly needs less gold. Net-debt-free, operationally strong miners (PAN, DRD) were sold alongside leveraged, indebted ones. This indiscriminate selling is the opportunity.
  • Stop-loss discipline confirmed: SOL's hard stop at R148 was triggered and executed. The discipline is not a failure — it is the system working. We preserved capital that is now being redeployed into better opportunities created by the same selloff.

Apr 11 factor scoring highlights: PAN scores 9/10 Value + 10/10 Catalyst (ZAR gold). DRD scores 9/10 Value + 10/10 Catalyst. Capitec scores 10/10 Quality — highest in the entire 600-stock universe. KIO scores 9/10 Value at P/E ~6×. NPN scores 8/10 on rand-hedge criteria.
