The US announced "Liberation Day" tariffs on April 2, initially paused for 90 days on most trading partners. However, China was explicitly excluded from the pause and tariffs were raised to 145%. This is the critical detail the market is still processing.

For SA equities specifically, the analysis breaks into three distinct categories. First, the Sasol (SOL) stop-loss at R148 has been triggered. Oil at $63 breaks the coal-to-liquids thesis — exit is not optional. The discipline of the stop-loss system is precisely what protects the portfolio against ruin. We take the loss, move on, and redeploy into better opportunities created by the same selloff.

Second, the gold price decline is a liquidity event, not a fundamental reset. At $3,100/oz and ZAR R19.2/USD, the ZAR gold price received by SA miners is ~R59,500/oz. Pan African Resources' all-in sustaining cost is ~R32,000/oz — an 85%+ operating margin that is higher in ZAR terms than it was at $4,700 gold in April before the ZAR shock. The market is confusing USD gold with ZAR gold. They are structurally different. This distinction is the alpha opportunity this week.

Third, new opportunities have been created: Capitec Bank is now at a compelling entry level after being sold off with everything else; Kumba Iron Ore (KIO) is at a historic P/E discount on iron ore demand fears — fears that are likely misplaced given China's historical response to US tariff shocks is domestic stimulus, not contraction. Anglo American Platinum (AMS) offers a critical minerals angle that the trade war has directly accelerated.

"The antifragile system does not panic — it recalculates. Every forced selloff is the market gifting you the positions it couldn't hold."

Key barbell action: Raise defensive core to 60%; enforce ≥45% rand-hedge mandate; exit SOL; add DRD gold (buy the dip), CPI, KIO, and AMS. Reduce moonshot to 5% given market conditions.

Trade-War-Aware Screening Methodology ✓ Free Preview

Our core multi-factor model is unchanged. Two new trade war overlays dominate this edition's screening:

  • Direct US Export Dependency Filter (new): SA exports only ~8% to the US vs ~15% to China and ~25% to EU. SA is materially less exposed to the US-China trade war than most EM peers. We penalise USD-denominated debt holders (rand at R19.2 means higher ZAR finance charges); we favour pure USD earners with ZAR cost bases (gold miners, Kumba, Naspers).
  • China Domestic Stimulus Anticipation: China's playbook in response to US tariffs has historically been: announce domestic infrastructure stimulus packages within 2–4 months. Infrastructure = iron ore. We position Kumba ahead of this announcement. This is the contrarian read that the market is missing.
  • Liquidity vs Fundamental Distinction (critical): Gold dropped from $4,700 to $3,100 in one week. This is not a fundamental repricing — it is margin-call liquidation by leveraged gold ETF holders. ZAR gold (what SA miners receive) is R59,500+/oz vs AISC of R30,000–35,000/oz. These margins are extraordinary and unchanged. Liquidation dips in operationally healthy, debt-free miners are historically the best buying opportunities. PAN and DRD qualify clearly.
  • ZAR Hedge Mandate raised to ≥45%: At R19.2/USD, purely domestic SA equity faces structural headwinds. We now require ≥45% of the portfolio to earn in USD-equivalent terms.
  • Stop-loss discipline validated: The SOL position hit its hard stop at R148. We exit without hesitation. The stop-loss system is what distinguishes antifragile from merely fragile portfolio construction.

Apr 10 factor highlights: PAN scores 10/10 Catalyst (ZAR gold thesis), 9/10 Value, 9/10 Quality (net-debt-free). DRD scores 10/10 Catalyst, 9/10 Value. Capitec scores 10/10 Quality — the highest quality score in the entire screen. Kumba scores 9/10 Value at current P/E ~6×.
