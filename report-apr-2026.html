02

Screening Methodology

We use a Barbell factor model combining value, quality, momentum, and catalysts. Academic evidence for predictive factors guides our screen. All candidate stocks must clear the following criteria:

  • Value: Low P/E or P/B versus peers and history (e.g. SHP P/E 18.8 vs retail peers; Growthpoint P/NAV 0.88). High free-cash-flow yields or dividend yields are a plus. Evidence: the value factor yields persistent returns.
  • Quality: Strong profitability (ROE/ROIC), stable earnings, low leverage. E.g. SBK ROE ~18% with healthy capital. Evidence: quality firms outperform over cycles.
  • Momentum/Technical: Upward price trends, RSI/MA signals, volume surges. We require at least moderate positive 3–6m momentum.
  • Catalysts (required): Each pick needs ≥1 clear catalyst in 3–12m: rate cuts, commodity upcycles, regulatory change, M&A, or earnings turnarounds.
  • Macro Overlay: SARB policy, rand FX, inflation, load-shedding. We favor cyclical longs if rates ease, and gold/miners if inflation stays low.
  • Liquidity: Minimum avg vol thresholds (>0.5m/day). Actual avg vol and bid-ask spread listed per pick. E.g. IOC ~0.87m vs 4Sight ~0.15m.
  • Other Signals: Insider buys/sells, institutional ownership, short interest (qualitative; data is sparse in SA).

Scoring Rubric (0–100): Each stock scored on (Value, Quality, Momentum, Liquidity, Catalysts), weighted to prefer undervalued + high catalyst probability. Examples: 4Sight scores high on value but low on liquidity; iOCO is balanced (value 7, growth 7).

Risk Analysis & Macro Overlay

Macro Risks — four key X-factors:

  • Inflation & Rates: A sudden jump (oil shock, war escalation) could force SARB hikes, hurting cyclicals. Stable 3% CPI and global ease (Fed paused) bode well for cuts by H2.
  • Rand Volatility: At ~17.1/USD, a sharp devaluation (>18.5) could negate equity gains and raise inflation.
  • Load-shedding: Currently negligible (EAF 65–69%). A relapse would hurt retailers and small-caps. We assume no major interruption through mid-2026.
  • Regulatory/Political: New taxes on retail, mining royalties, or fintech regulation. Monitor any policy shifts closely.

Stock-specific risks: Banks — credit cycle spike, deposit flight. NPN/MTN — tech regulatory crackdowns. Shoprite — consumer spending squeeze. Growthpoint — property oversupply. iOCO/4Sight — execution risk. Pan African — gold price reversal.

We mitigate via stops or hedges (e.g. index puts to guard against portfolio tail risk). In a "bear" scenario (geopolitical shock, rand crash), defensive banks and JSE Ltd hold value (+5–10%), while small tech/gold names could drop 30–50%. The barbell ensures ~40–50% of portfolio in lower-volatility core.
