The Israel-Lebanon 10-day ceasefire was announced on April 17, with Iran's maritime authorities confirming the Strait of Hormuz remains open to commercial shipping (via a "coordinated route"). Oil fell more than 10% on the news, resetting what had been a significant oil-driven inflation shock. This single event has changed the macro calculus considerably:

  • SARB rate cut path restored: With oil falling and the primary inflationary shock easing, Standard Bank's guidance of 2 cuts in 2026 (May and September, 75bps total) looks increasingly credible again. This is a direct positive for banks, REITs, and consumer stocks.
  • Gold recovery is NOT the same as the original peak: When gold was $4,700 on April 8, ZAR was R16.9 → ZAR gold was R79,430/oz. Today gold is $4,867, ZAR is R16.35 → ZAR gold is R79,575/oz. SA miners are receiving virtually identical ZAR gold prices despite all the volatility. The thesis has held throughout every regime shift.
  • ZAR gold margin is unchanged: PAN's AISC ~R32,000/oz vs ZAR gold R79,500/oz = 148% operating margin. This is the highest in PAN's history and is not materially different from the pre-April 9 position.
  • Gold all-time high context: Gold's record is ~$5,602/oz (January 28, 2026). At $4,867, gold remains 13% below that level — meaning there is still substantial upside room in the USD gold price before any reversal to mean.

The ceasefire is described as fragile — the situation in the region remains complex, with Iran and the US agreeing to prolong nuclear negotiations. A re-escalation remains possible, which is why maintaining gold miners as a core portfolio allocation remains appropriate.

"When the smoke clears, the antifragile portfolio is always better positioned than it was before. The April 9–10 forced liquidation was the market gifting us entry prices at PAN and DRD that we may not see again for years."

The Capitec Catalyst: April 22, 2026

This is the single most immediate catalyst in the portfolio. Capitec (CPI) reports full-year results for the year ending February 28, 2026 on April 22 — four days from today. The company already guided in February 2026 that headline earnings per share (HEPS) would be between R14,294 and R14,890 per share — growth of 20–25% on the prior year's 11,912c.

The stock currently sits at ~R4,182, approximately 12% below its all-time high of R4,833. This pullback — caused by the indiscriminate trade war selloff — against a backdrop of record guided earnings creates a compelling pre-results entry window. ROE of 30%+ (highest in SA banking) combined with 25 million clients and the structural trend of mass-market banking consolidation make Capitec the most compelling quality-growth pick in the SA financial sector.

Recovery-Aware Screening Methodology ✓ Free Preview

Our multi-factor framework is unchanged. Three specific updates have been applied to the screening engine based on lessons from Editions 03–04:

  • GOLD_RECOVERY_VELOCITY rule (new, permanent): When gold drops >15% in <5 days due to forced liquidation (margin call signatures: rapid fall, correlated with equities, reversal within 1–2 weeks), treat it as a buying event for net-debt-free SA gold miners. Do NOT exit ZAR-denominated gold miners based on USD gold alone. The ZAR gold price is the operative variable for SA miners. This rule has now been added permanently to the rules engine after its comprehensive validation in this episode.
  • CEASEFIRE_OIL_SARB cascade (new): Middle East ceasefire → oil falls 10%+ → SARB inflation forecast improves → rate cut probability increases → overweight banks and REITs. This cascade operates in the reverse direction of the April 9 oil shock. We now flag it as a regime trigger in its own right, affecting our bank thesis positively.
  • PRE_RESULTS_WINDOW rule (codified): When a stock in our portfolio has upcoming results within 10 days and the guided earnings growth is >15%, we classify this as a TIER-1 CATALYST. The pre-results window is when smart money accumulates. Do not reduce position ahead of results unless stop-loss is threatened. This applies to CPI on April 22.
  • ZAR gold calculation — mandatory field: Every gold miner in the screen now includes: (a) USD gold price, (b) ZAR/USD rate, (c) ZAR gold = (a)×(b), (d) ZAR AISC, (e) ZAR operating margin = (c−d)/c. This is now a permanent methodology upgrade.
  • Harmony Gold HAR — entry criteria met: HAR has the highest operating leverage to gold of any major JSE miner. At $4,867 gold and R16.35/USD, HAR receives ~R79,500/oz. Their AISC is approximately R45,000–50,000/oz, leaving ~60% operating margins at scale. HAR enters the screen as the gold price recovery edition's highest-leverage new pick.
  • KIO China window tracker: The China domestic stimulus thesis has a 4-month window from April 10. We are now at week 5. Historical precedent suggests 60–120 days from tariff escalation to stimulus announcement. We are at ~40 days. The window remains open. Monitor weekly for PBOC or NDRC infrastructure announcements.
